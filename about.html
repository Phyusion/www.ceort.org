About CEORT

Our Story & Mission

For over two decades, the CEO Roundtable on Cancer has united the nation's top corporate leaders in the fight to eliminate cancer as a major health threat.

Our Mission

Eliminating Cancer Through Leadership

The mission of the CEO Roundtable on Cancer is to make continual progress toward the elimination of cancer as a personal disease and public health problem through initiatives that:

  • Reduce the risk of cancer
  • Enable early diagnosis and detection
  • Facilitate access to treatment and clinical trials
  • Hasten the discovery of effective therapies
  • Promote health equity across all communities
Our History

A Legacy of Bold Action

From a presidential challenge to a nationwide movement, the CEO Roundtable on Cancer has steadily expanded its impact on cancer prevention and treatment.

2001

Presidential Challenge

President George H.W. Bush challenges Robert A. Ingram, then CEO of GlaxoWellcome, to convene and chair a roundtable of CEOs to "do something bold and venturesome about cancer." The CEO Roundtable on Cancer is born.

2001

CEO Cancer Gold Standard Created

The founding CEOs respond by creating the CEO Cancer Gold Standard—an evidence-based workplace wellness accreditation framework focused on cancer risk reduction, early detection, and quality care.

2005

501(c)(3) Incorporation

The CEO Roundtable on Cancer is officially chartered as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization, formalizing its structure and expanding its ability to drive industry-wide change.

2014

Project Data Sphere Launches

Project Data Sphere is launched as an independent initiative to break down barriers to clinical trial data sharing, creating an open-access platform for patient-level cancer research data.

2024

PACT Collective Announced

CEORT and Paradigm Health launch the Partnership for Access, Collaboration, and Trust (PACT) Collective to expand clinical trial access and diversity in underserved communities.

2024

Going for Gold Initiative

The Going for Gold initiative launches to partner Gold Standard companies with HBCUs and minority-serving institutions, with the capacity to impact 20+ million lives within 7–10 years.
